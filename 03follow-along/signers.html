Who Were the Signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

John Adams
A leader in advocating for independence, he later became the 2nd President of the United States and was instrumental in drafting the Declaration.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, diplomat, and inventor, he played a key role in securing French support for the revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, he is famous for his large and prominent signature on the Declaration.
Samuel Adams
A major figure in organizing the Boston Tea Party and a vocal advocate for independence from Britain.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress, which led to the drafting of the Declaration.
Robert Morris
Known as the "Financier of the Revolution," he played a critical role in funding the war effort.
Francis Lightfoot Lee
A member of the prominent Lee family of Virginia, he contributed to the independence movement in the colonies.
Elbridge Gerry
Known for his later role as Vice President and for the term "gerrymandering," which stems from his political actions.
George Wythe
A mentor to Thomas Jefferson and a key legal mind of the revolution, he was the first professor of law in America.

Each of these induividuals played a significant role in shaping the Declaration of Independence and its impact on the history of the United States. They are remembered for their courage for signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
